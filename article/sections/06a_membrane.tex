

With the continuum framework established, the constitutive equations defined, and the material parameters identified, this section addresses the numerical verification and application of the developed formulation. The non-linear weak form and finite element framework detailed in Section \ref{sec:FEM residuals} are deployed to simulate a series of representative multi-field initial-boundary value problems (IBVPs).

\subsection{Numerical verification of pre-stretched dielectric elastomers: a planar actuator benchmark}

The primary technical objectives of this numerical example are:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item To verify the algorithmic robustness, stability of the proposed Total Lagrangian Finite Element (FEM) formulation under coupled thermoelectromechanical loading, finite strain and pre-stretched conditions.
    \item To assess the performance of the calibrated thermo-electro-mechanical constitutive model under different long-term hyper-elastic components.
    \item To benchmark the numerical results (e.g., radial stretch $\lambda_1$ and out-of-plane displacement) against experimental data available in the literature.
    \item To assess the thermodynamic consistency of the coupled scheme under cyclic voltage actuation, isolating the cyclic and monotonic contributions to the self-heating response and tracking the associated evolution of global energetic quantities (internal energy, dissipation, entropy).
    \item To verify the material stability of the calibrated model at finite pre-stretch by evaluating directional projections of the acoustic tensor.
\end{enumerate}

The problem geometry consists of a thin square membrane, depicted in Figure~\ref{fig:membrane_geometry}. The elastomeric film is subjected to a homogeneous equibiaxial pre-stretch and then fixed within a rigid square frame; the pre-stretch and the undeformed thickness fix the out-of-plane thickness at simulation start. A compliant electrode is then applied to the upper surface, covering a centered square domain spanning half the frame width (Figure~\ref{fig:membrane_geometry}). Simulations use the calibrated parameters for VHB 4910 (Section~\ref{sec:calibration}), with initial temperature \qty{20}{\degreeCelsius}. Table~\ref{tab:membrane_parameters} lists the geometry and loading parameters; pre-stretch $\lambda_\text{p}$ and applied voltage are varied over the ranges indicated.

The pre-stretch is imposed as a fixed multiplicative deformation $\F_\text{p} = \text{diag}(\lambda_\text{p},\lambda_\text{p},\lambda_\text{p}^{-2})$, incompressible and composed with the FE-driven deformation gradient, $\F = (\I+\nabla\vect{u})\F_\text{p}$. This composition enters the residual and its consistent tangent through the pull-back of the test and trial function gradients by $\F_\text{p}$ and the associated Jacobian $J(\F_\text{p})$, and it fixes the reference state at which the internal (viscous) variables are initialized, prior to any electromechanical loading.

This configuration is comparable to the classic circular DEA benchmark, enabling a qualitative assessment of the constitutive framework, while its Cartesian profile admits a structured mesh of uniform aspect ratio. Quadratic elements are used throughout to avoid locking at finite strain.

The calibrated model is confronted with third-party experimental data at $\lambda_\text{p}=3$ (Figure~\ref{fig:literature_comparison}): the predicted stretch-voltage response matches the reported measurements closely, closing the verification-to-validation loop for this benchmark.

\subsubsection{Thermodynamic consistency and self-heating under cyclic voltage actuation}

The same geometry, at fixed $\lambda_\text{p}=3$, is now driven by a sinusoidal voltage of amplitude \qty{5}{\kV} at \qty{10}{\Hz} over about 50 cycles. The average temperature shows two superposed effects, an order of magnitude apart: a cyclic modulation synchronized with the electric loading ($\sim$\qty{0.01}{\celsius}), and a monotonic drift from cumulative viscous dissipation ($\sim$\qty{0.001}{\celsius} over the 50 cycles). Resolving both simultaneously exercises the sensitivity of the numerical scheme and the thermodynamic consistency of the formulation.

\begin{figure}
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{membrane/temperature_time.png}
  \caption{Temperature history over ~100 voltage cycles at $\lambda_p=3$: raw signal (cyclic ripple, $\sim$\qty{0.01}{\celsius}) with the monotonic self-heating trend ($\sim$\qty{0.001}{\celsius} over 50 cycles) highlighted separately, e.g. via a moving-average overlay or a decomposed twin-panel plot.}
\end{figure}


The framework also gives direct access to global thermodynamic quantities not observable experimentally: the internal energy rate $\dot\Psi_u+\dot\Psi_\varphi+\dot\Psi_\theta$, the aligned free-energy contribution $\Psi_{\varphi,\text{dir}}$, and the viscous dissipation power $\Dvis$. Their evolution over the loading cycles is consistent with the temperature response above, with $\Dvis$ alone accounting for the secular drift.

  \caption{Maximum stretch evolution. \emph{(left)} The voltage-stretch hystersis shows a shift with increasing stretches, related to viscous dissipation. \emph{(right)} Time evolution of the stretch and global energetic quantities, such as the viscous dissipation $\Dvis$.}
\end{figure}



\subsubsection{Material stability: acoustic tensor projections}

Finally, material stability at $\lambda_\text{p}=3$ is checked by evaluating the acoustic tensor over the full range of propagation directions $(\alpha,\beta)$. No vanishing or negative eigenvalues are found, confirming strong ellipticity is retained in the pre-stretched, energized state used throughout this benchmark.
