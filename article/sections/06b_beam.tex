
\subsection{Thermo-mechanical coupling and viscous dissipation in highly anisotropic plate-beams: an insect-scale wing-like structural benchmark}

The primary technical objectives of this second numerical example are:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item To assess the algorithmic robustness of the formulation under large-deformation flexural and torsional kinematics.
    \item To evaluate the performance of the anisotropic constitutive model, using an elongated span-to-width aspect ratio that amplifies the anisotropy-induced bending-torsion coupling and renders it clearly visible.
    \item To quantify the effects of viscous dissipation on material self-heating and analyze the resulting multi-field thermo-mechanical coupling under cyclic loading.
    \item To verify the material stability of the anisotropic model under the combined bending-torsion state by evaluating directional projections of the acoustic tensor.
\end{enumerate}

The anisotropic response is introduced through a transverse-isotropy invariant term added to the isotropic hyperelastic energy, built on the structural tensor of a single fiber direction oriented at \qty{45}{\degree} in-plane. This off-axis alignment is what couples bending and torsion under normal actuation; the invariant formulation itself follows \cite{XX}.

\subsubsection{Material stability: acoustic tensor projections}

The acoustic tensor is evaluated over the full range of propagation directions $(\alpha,\beta)$ at the state of maximum bending-torsion coupling. Strong ellipticity is retained under this combined flexural-torsional, anisotropic loading, a stricter test than the pre-stretched state checked in the planar actuator example.
