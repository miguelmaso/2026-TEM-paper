
\subsection{Thermo-mechanical coupling and viscous dissipation in highly anisotropic plate-beams: an insect-scale wing-like structural benchmark}

The primary technical objectives of this second numerical example are fivefold:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item To assess the algorithmic robustness and convergence behavior of the formulation under large-deformation flexural and torsional kinematics exhibiting severe geometric non-linearities.
    \item To evaluate the performance of the anisotropic constitutive model in capturing strong directional stiffness variations and complex structural couplings (e.g., bending-torsion coupling).
    \item To quantify the effects of viscous dissipation on material self-heating and analyze the resulting multi-field thermo-mechanical feedback under cyclic or dynamic states.
    \item To validate the capability of the numerical framework to handle complex boundary conditions and load distributions mimicking bio-inspired kinematics without loss of computational rigor.
    \item To conduct a systematic space-time discretization sensitivity study to ensure mesh-independent and stable capturing of localized stress, temperature, and energy dissipation fields.
\end{enumerate}
